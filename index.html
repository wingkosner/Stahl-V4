Fourth Edition 2013

Better. Faster. Smaller.

BUY IT HERE!

This is the textbook that started it all, now in its fourth edition.

It tells you how diseases act in the brain, and how drugs act on those diseases. With its use of icons and figures that form the unique ‘visual language’ for psychopharmacology which has become a signature feature of all of Dr Stahl’s books, it is the single most readily readable source of information on disease and drug mechanisms for psychiatrists, psychologists, primary care physicians, pharmacists and other health professionals, in training and in practice.

The fourth edition represents the most radical update this book has ever had. In addition to being thoroughly updated the book has been slimmed down, with redundancies and older material taken out, and a new larger page size and two column layout making the ‘essentials’ even easier to access than before.

The Prescriber’s Guide

Fourth Edition

BUY IT HERE!

Not to be confused with the textbook, which tells you how diseases act in the brain and how drugs act on the diseases, the Prescriber’s Guide tells you how to use the drugs.

Indexed by name, use, and class, around one hundred different drugs are covered, each in its own section with information on indications, the drugs, and how to use them. In addition to the standard evidence-based drug information, ‘tips and pearls’ based on the art of pharmacology derived as much from Dr Stahl’s real life experience as a practicing psychiatrist as from the evidence are included, and characteristic use is made of unique icons.

Frequently updated to include new drugs and revised to take account of new regulations and uses, this is an indispensable companion to Stahl’s Essential Psychopharmacology.

Self Assessment

Examination in Psychaitry

BUY IT HERE!

Are you up to date?

This small book is a tool that can be used not only during training but also afterwards in practice to help you assess how up to date you are, and to identify areas of competence or the need for further study.

In addition, the 150 questions themselves are learning exercises: each is followed with by a thorough explanation of the correct and incorrect answer choices. Readers can work through the questions – derived from Dr Stahl’s Online Master Psychopharmacology Program – to prepare for formal tests, including the American Board of Psychiatry and Neurology examinations and to achieve CME and MoC credits towards ongoing ABPN re-accreditation.

Stahl’s Online

The Complete Digital Resource

Stahl’s Essential Psychopharmacology Online features the full text and all the illustrations from Stahl’s Essential Psychopharmacology, The Prescriber’s Guide, books from the Stahl’s Illustrated Series, and other books authored and co-authored by Dr Stahl, all searchable on the same platform.

  • Search drugs by class, name, type, or use across all content, then easily filter your results
  • Use quick search for easy use during patient appointments
  • Download illustrations and tables for presentations and lectures
  • Cite, bookmark or take notes at the click of a button
  • Link directly to suggested readings
  • Use one-click access to the Neuroscience Education Institute’s CME portal

Updates and related content added several times each year.

Available on an annual subscription and pay-per-view basis for individuals and with special licensing rates for institutional and corporate libraries.

CNS Spectrums

The Journal of the Neuroscience Education Institute

After being acquired by Cambridge Journals in November 2011, CNS Spectrums was reconfigured and re-launched in March 2012.

Building upon its previous status, the new Editor-in-Chief, Dr Stephen M Stahl, formed a new editorial board and developed the aims and scope of the journal to fully bridge the clinical information needs of psychiatrists and neurologists.

Now moving from a publication rate of four issues plus a CME issue in 2012 to six issues plus a CME issue for each of the years 2013 and 2014, CNS Spectrums is going from strength to strength.

The journal has an open solicitation for review articles and original data, and we invite you to participate.

More at journals.cambridge.org/cns.
